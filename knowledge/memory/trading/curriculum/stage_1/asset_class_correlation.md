## 2026-03-07 — knowledge/memory/trading/curriculum/stage_1/intermarket_analysis.md

Intermarket analysis examines correlations between stocks, bonds, commodities, and currencies to predict market movements, with currency diversification emphasized as essential protection against government-induced inflation. Forex markets serve as early warning signals for other asset classes, while three types of FX risk (transaction, translation, economic) require hedging through forwards, options, and natural hedging strategies. Correlation relationships are dynamic, shifting based on inflationary versus deflationary environments and requiring continuous monitoring.

**Key concepts:** Intermarket analysis, Asset class correlation, Currency diversification, Inflation hedge, Carry trade, Transaction risk, Translation risk, Economic risk, Foreign exchange risk, Hedging, Forward contracts, Options, Natural hedging, Trading sessions, Liquidity, Risk-adjusted returns, Positive correlation, Negative correlation, Inverse price/yield relationship

**Trading implications:**
- Maintain multi-currency exposure to hedge against government-induced inflation and debt devaluation
- Use forex markets as early warning signals for other asset classes
- Trade during London-New York session overlap for peak liquidity and volatility
- Exploit correlation shifts between inflationary and deflationary environments
- Hedge FX risk through forwards, options, and natural hedging strategies
- Avoid concentrated single-currency exposure as a 'fatal error'
- Utilize carry trades for risk-adjusted returns
- Monitor correlation changes continuously as they are dynamic not fixed

**Risk factors:**
- Government-induced inflation and debt devaluation
- Correlation instability in emerging markets or minor currencies due to low liquidity
- Temporary correlation structure changes during market instability
- Geopolitical risks affecting multiple markets simultaneously
- Unintentional risk concentration from overlooking inverse correlations (e.g., USDJPY and EURUSD)
- Fixed income inflation and interest rate risks
- Leverage risks in forex trading
