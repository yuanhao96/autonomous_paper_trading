These documents synthesize fundamental valuation metrics—particularly EPS (basic and diluted) and P/E ratios (trailing, forward, absolute, relative)—with technical analysis methodologies rooted in Dow Theory. Key themes include using EPS calculations as critical inputs for valuation, applying trailing P/E for mature companies and forward P/E for growth assessment, while complementing these with trend analysis, support/resistance levels, and breakout strategies for market timing.

**Key concepts:** Basic EPS, Diluted EPS, Trailing P/E (TTM/LTM), Forward P/E, Weighted Average Shares Outstanding, Convertible Securities, Dow Theory, Trend Analysis, Support and Resistance, Breakouts, PEG Ratio, Earnings Yield, Relative P/E, Market Breadth, Advance-Decline Line, Sentiment Analysis, Contrary Opinion

**Trading implications:**
- Use trailing P/E for mature companies with stable earnings history
- Use forward P/E for growth assessment and future profitability evaluation
- Enter positions on confirmed breakouts with volume confirmation
- Place protective stops below support level
- Combine fundamental valuation with technical timing for entry/exit decisions

**Risk factors:**
- Dilution risk from convertible securities not captured in basic EPS
- Forward P/E relies on analyst estimates which may be inaccurate
- Technical breakouts can fail without volume confirmation
- Currency depreciation risk from government inflation policies
- Over-reliance on single valuation metric without complementary analysis
