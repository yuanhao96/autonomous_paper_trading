## 2026-03-02 — Technical Analysis - The Complete Resource for Financial Market Technicians 2nd edition 2010 — part 2

These documents cover foundational technical analysis concepts centered on Dow Theory, market structure, trend analysis, and sentiment measurement. The materials span from the original 19th-century principles developed by Charles Dow through modern applications including chart construction, breadth indicators, and behavioral finance integration with fundamental metrics like P/E ratios.

**Key concepts:** Dow Theory, Primary/Secondary/Minor Trends, Trend Analysis, Support and Resistance, Market Breadth, Advance-Decline Line, Sentiment Analysis, Contrary Opinion, Point-and-Figure Charts, Candlestick Charts, Breakouts, Trailing/Forward P/E, PEG Ratio, Arms Index, Hindenburg Omen, Confirmation Concept, Volume Analysis

**Trading implications:**
- Use Dow Theory trend classification to identify primary market direction for position sizing
- Apply trailing P/E for mature companies and forward P/E for growth companies in stock selection
- Monitor advance-decline line divergences for early trend reversal warnings
- Use sentiment indicators (put/call ratios, polls) as contrarian signals at extremes
- Confirm breakouts with volume and wait for retracement entries to improve risk/reward
- Employ multiple time frame analysis aligning trades with primary trend direction
- Use protective and trailing stops based on support/resistance levels to manage risk

**Risk factors:**
- Dow Theory signals often lag significant market moves
- Traditional advance-decline methods have become less profitable over time
- No central authority exists to synchronize Dow Theory analyst signals
- Sentiment indicators can remain extreme for extended periods
- Breakout failures and false signals occur frequently
- Volume patterns have evolved with market structure changes
- Behavioral biases can distort both fundamental and technical analysis
- Reliance on single indicators increases false signal risk
